## Project Abstract

Phytoplankton biomass and water-column total phosphorus (TP) concentrations are reliable indicators of trophic status in lakes where most P resides in planktonic biomass. However, these sestonic metrics lose effectiveness in P-limited shallow ecosystems dominated by benthic algal and macrophyte producers. Measurements of water-column TP in shallow freshwater ecosystems alone do not fully capture TP concentration and availability and can lead to misidentifying water body trophic status. Nevertheless, sestonic metrics continue to be used to assess trophic status in shallow ecosystems, largely in the absence of more appropriate tools. We conducted a literature review to characterize the strength and forms of relationships between water-column TP and biotic TP, and the biomass of phytoplankton, benthic algae, and macrophytes across P-limited shallow lentic ecosystems including lakes, ponds, and wetlands. Phytoplankton and benthic algal biomass increased with higher water-column TP concentrations in littoral zones of deeper lakes and in shallow lakes and ponds. In contrast, benthic algal biomass in wetlands was weakly negatively correlated with water-column TP, but strongly positively correlated with benthic microbial TP standing stock (g m-3); a similar positive relationship was observed in all other shallow lentic ecosystems. To reconcile the contrasting patterns of water-column vs. TP standing stock relationship with producer biomass, we evaluated benthic microbial and macrophyte biomass along a TP gradient in two karstic wetlands, where benthic algal biomass declines with increasing water-column TP. In the karstic wetlands, there was a positive linear relationship between benthic microbial biomass and microbial TP standing stock (g m-3) as well as macrophyte biomass and macrophyte TP stock (g m-2). Using either benthic microbial or macrophyte TP standing stock for assessing trophic status in wetlands and other shallow freshwater ecosystems is therefore comparable to sestonic approaches in lakes, requires similar effort, and more accurately reflects ecosystem production capacity.

## Manuscript Highlights

- Water-column total phosphorus (TP) is not a reliable indicator of benthic chlorophyll-a production in P-limited shallow freshwater ecosystems.
- Instead, benthic producer phosphorus standing stock forms a predicable positive relationship with benthic algal chlorophyll-a and macrophyte biomass. 
- This benthic biomass-TP standing stock relationship can be used to develop a trophic status classification system for shallow lentic ecosystems like wetlands.
